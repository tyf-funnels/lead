About Me

// The person doing the work

I'm Anes Hass — a designer and developer whose work is scoped to one problem: SaaS landing pages that don't convert at the rate the acquisition budget demands. I've spent years working through why high-intent visitors leave before the CTA — and the answer is almost always the same three structural failures. The above-the-fold section doesn't communicate value to a cold visitor in time to hold attention. The content sequence reflects internal product logic instead of how a skeptical buyer evaluates a new tool. And the CTA appears before the page has neutralized the visitor's default objection. Those three things are what I fix. Everything else is a consequence of getting those right.

What I Do

// Core scope

Landing Pages Rebuilt Around How Buyers Actually Evaluate SaaS

A prospect landing on your page for the first time has one implicit question running in parallel with everything they read: "Is this worth the friction of signing up?" The page needs to answer that question before they've decided to leave. I rebuild the full page — structure, hierarchy, messaging, and CTA sequencing — so that every section advances the buyer toward that decision rather than interrupting it.

// The four structural levers

Where Most SaaS Pages Break — and What Changes When They Don't

Above-the-fold clarity under 5 seconds — headline, subheadline, and primary CTA rebuilt so a cold visitor immediately understands what you do, for whom, and why it's worth the next scroll.

Message hierarchy that follows the buyer's sequence — content ordered to match how a skeptical evaluator moves from problem recognition to proof of fit to objection resolution.

Objection removal before the CTA — the specific friction points preventing signup are addressed in the page body before the visitor reaches the action step.

Load performance that doesn't waste acquisition spend — pages delivered under 2 seconds so paid traffic isn't lost at the browser's rendering step.

How It Works

Structured benchmark — you evaluate the work before any commitment

I Rebuild Your Page. You Compare It Against Your Live Version. Then You Decide.

Before any contract or scope agreement, I take your current landing page and rebuild its core structure around a single objective: reducing the gap between a visitor arriving and them initiating a trial. You receive a fully functional coded page — not a static mockup, not a Figma file — that you can place directly beside your live version and evaluate on measurable criteria. If the rebuilt version isn't a clear structural improvement, you leave with a documented diagnosis of what's broken and no invoice.

// What the benchmark includes
  • ► Information architecture restructure — content reordered to match the decision stages a first-time buyer moves through: problem relevance, product fit, credibility evidence, risk reduction, action. Each section earns its position in that sequence.
  • ► Above-the-fold rewrite — headline, subheadline, and primary CTA rebuilt to communicate your specific value proposition to a cold, skeptical visitor in under 5 seconds. No jargon, no feature lists above the fold.
  • ► Friction-point diagnosis — the specific structural and messaging failures causing pre-CTA exits, identified from your current page and explained in language that maps to a concrete fix, not a list of best practices.
  • ► A deployable alternative — working, production-ready code. You can ship it, A/B test it against your live page, or hand it to your team. This is a functional deliverable, not a consultation document.
