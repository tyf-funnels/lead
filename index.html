About

// The person behind the work

I'm Anes Hass — a conversion designer and developer who works exclusively with online coaches and course creators running high-ticket offers. My focus is narrow by design: landing pages that close Instagram-warm traffic.

Here's what I've seen repeatedly. A coach builds a genuinely valuable program — $2K, $5K, sometimes more — posts content that earns real trust, drives consistent traffic to a landing page, and watches that traffic leave without buying. The offer isn't the problem. The page is. It was built for strangers. It explains the program instead of enrolling the reader. It lists modules instead of resolving doubt. It asks for the sale before it's earned the right to ask.

That is a solvable problem. And it's the only problem I solve. I audit your existing page, identify exactly where trust breaks and buyers exit, then rebuild every section to speak directly to the person who just came from your content — and convert them.

The Real Problem

// Why good offers lose sales

Your Page Is Describing Your Offer. It Should Be Enrolling Your Reader.

A description tells people what they get. An enrollment page makes them feel what changes when they have it. Most pages written by the coach — or a generalist copywriter — land in "feature list" territory. Every section I write is built around a single question the visitor is silently asking at that exact moment in the page. Answer the right question at the right time, and resistance drops. Drop resistance, and the buy button gets clicked.

// What a rebuilt page does differently

Four Conversion Levers Your Current Page Is Almost Certainly Missing

An identity-locked headline — the right person should read your headline and think "this is exactly for me." Right now, your headline probably describes the outcome. It should identify the person.

Objection resolution built into the body copy — "Is this worth $X for someone at my level?" lives in your reader's head. Your page has to answer it before the CTA appears, not after.

A trust arc that mirrors the Instagram relationship — your audience already trusts you from content. The page needs to honor that, not reset it. The structure, tone, and copy all need to feel like a natural continuation of your brand — not a generic sales page.

Technical credibility that holds attention — slow pages and cluttered layouts signal "not serious." Every page I deliver loads in under 2 seconds on mobile and is built to retain attention, not fracture it.
