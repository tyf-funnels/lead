About

// The person behind the work

I'm Anes Hass — a conversion designer and developer focused exclusively on coaches and course creators running high-ticket offers. My work is deliberately narrow: landing pages that convert warm, Instagram-sourced traffic into paying clients.

Here's what I see constantly: a coach builds a genuinely valuable program — $2K, $5K, more. They earn real trust through their content. They drive consistent traffic to a landing page. And that page loses them. Not because the offer is weak — because the page was written for cold strangers. It lists modules instead of resolving doubt. It describes the program instead of enrolling the reader. It asks for the sale before earning the right to ask.

That's a solvable problem. And it's the only problem I solve. I audit your page, identify exactly where trust breaks and buyers exit, then rebuild every section to speak directly to someone who just came from your content — someone who's warm, already curious, and just needs the page to do its job.

The Real Problem

// Why strong offers lose sales

Your Page Is Describing Your Offer. It Should Be Enrolling Your Reader.

A description tells visitors what they get. An enrollment page makes them feel what changes when they have it. Most pages land in "feature list" territory — written by the coach or a generalist. Every section I write is anchored to a single question the visitor is silently asking at that exact moment. Answer the right question at the right moment, and resistance drops. Drop resistance, and the buy button gets clicked.

// What a rebuilt page does differently

Four Things Your Page Is Almost Certainly Missing

An identity-locked headline — your ideal buyer should read the first line and think "this was written for me." Most headlines describe an outcome. They should identify the person.

Objections answered before the CTA — "Is this right for me?" lives in every reader's head. Your page needs to resolve that before asking for the sale.

A trust arc that honors the relationship — your audience already trusts you from months of content. The page should mirror that — not reset it cold.

Technical credibility — slow loads and cluttered layouts signal "not serious." Every page I deliver loads under two seconds on mobile and is built to hold attention.
