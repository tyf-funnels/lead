About

// The person behind the work

I'm Anes Hass — a web designer focused on one thing: helping local businesses get more customers from the website they already have. I work with salons, clinics, dental offices, gyms, law firms, and other service businesses that are losing leads every day because their page doesn't make it easy enough to call or book.

Here's the situation I see all the time: a business owner spends money on ads or relies on Google search to drive traffic. People land on the page. And then they leave — without calling, without booking, without doing anything. Not because the business isn't good. Because the page is confusing, slow on mobile, or doesn't tell the visitor what to do next.

That's a fixable problem — and it's the only problem I fix. I look at your current page, identify exactly what's stopping visitors from taking action, and rebuild it in 48 hours. Faster loading, clearer message, an obvious next step. You see it before you pay. If it's not better, you owe nothing.

The Real Problem

// Why your page isn't converting

Your Page Has Traffic. It Just Isn't Turning That Traffic Into Calls.

Most local business pages have the same issue: they tell visitors what the business does, but they don't give the visitor a clear, confident reason to call right now. No urgency. No obvious next step. A button buried at the bottom. On mobile, it's even worse — slow loading, tiny text, a phone number you have to copy-paste. Every one of those friction points costs you a customer. I remove all of them.

// What a page that converts actually does

Four Things Most Local Business Pages Are Missing

A headline that speaks to the customer, not the business — the first line should make a visitor think "yes, this is exactly what I need." Most headlines just say the business name.

One clear action, front and center — call, book, or message. Not three options. Not buried. Right there, immediately visible.

Answers to the questions every visitor has — "Is this place good? Do they serve my area? How much does it cost?" Unresolved questions mean they go to your competitor.

A page that actually works on a phone — over 80% of your visitors are on mobile. A slow, cluttered page on mobile loses the sale before they read a single word.
