About

// The person behind the work

I'm Anes Hass — a web designer who builds landing pages exclusively for salons, clinics, and local service businesses. I focus on one outcome: turning your existing website traffic into booked appointments and calls.

The pattern I see constantly: a salon owner runs Instagram ads or relies on Google to drive traffic. People land on the page. And then they leave — without booking, without calling. Not because the business isn't great. Because the page is slow on mobile, the booking button is buried, and there's no clear reason to act right now.

That's a fixable problem — and it's the only problem I fix. I review your current page, identify exactly what's losing you bookings, and rebuild it in 48 hours. You see it before you pay. If it's not better, you owe nothing.

The Real Problem

// Why your page isn't converting

Your Salon Has Traffic. It Just Isn't Turning Visitors Into Bookings.

Most salon and clinic pages share the same problem: they show what the business offers, but give the visitor no compelling reason to book right now. No urgency. No obvious next step. A booking link buried at the bottom. On mobile — where most of your clients are searching — it's even worse: slow to load, hard to read, a number they have to manually copy. Every friction point loses you a client. I remove all of them.

// What a page that books clients actually does

Four Things Most Salon & Clinic Pages Are Missing

A headline that speaks to the client, not the business — the first line should make someone think "this is exactly the place I want." Most pages just say the salon name.

One clear booking action, immediately visible — call, book, or DM. Not three options. Not scrolled to. Right at the top, on every device.

Answers to what every new client wants to know — "Are they good? Do they serve my area? What are the prices?" Unanswered questions send them to a competitor.

A page built for phones, not desktops — over 80% of your clients search on mobile. A slow, cluttered mobile page loses the booking before they've read a single line.
